### Pollinator Strategy for Clover Domes (First-Principles Rationale)
Clover Domes feature stands of **non-spreading (clumping) giant bamboo** (e.g., *Fargesia* or select *Bambusa* cultivars that expand only inches per year — ideal for controlled, non-invasive vertical structure, biomass, and habitat without rhizome takeover). These provide structural support, shade, edible shoots (in select varieties), and microhabitats.

**Edible flowers and fruits** (e.g., wild strawberries, mulberries, monk fruit vines, borage, calendula, squash blossoms, etc.) increase gourmet yield, nutrition, and pollinator forage while supporting polyculture.

**Honeybees** remain primary but are supplemented for redundancy (disease, temperature swings, low-pressure effects, or colony stress in domes). Diversity defeats single-point failure — a core first-principles requirement for multiplanetary resilience.

### Recommended Additional Pollinators (Mars Dome Compatible)
All selected for:
- Enclosed environment suitability (manageable populations, low escape risk).
- No chemical pesticides (biological controls + polyculture).
- Multi-function symbiosis (pollination + food + waste cycling + morale).
- Optimus/Grok management (hive/nest monitoring, health optimization).
- Low-mass initial transport + self-sustaining breeding.

1. **Bumblebees (*Bombus* spp.)** — Top recommendation.  
   Excellent for buzz pollination (critical for tomatoes, potatoes, peppers, some fruits). Work in cooler/lower-light conditions common in early domes. Used successfully in commercial greenhouses. Short-lived colonies reduce long-term management. Manure/waste integration via Leg 2.

2. **Mason Bees (e.g., *Osmia* spp., Blue Orchard Bees)** — High-efficiency.  
   Solitary, gentle, 2–3x more effective pollinators than honeybees per visit. Excellent for fruits (strawberries, mulberries) and flowers. Nest in simple reed/wood tubes or 3D-printed blocks — easy Optimus scaling. Low disease transmission risk.

3. **Leafcutter Bees (*Megachile* spp.)** — Robust for legumes/clover.  
   Strong pollinators for clover, alfalfa-like crops, and many edibles. Cut leaves for nesting (provides natural pruning). Manageable in enclosed setups.

4. **Hoverflies / Syrphid Flies** — Non-bee backup.  
   Larvae are excellent aphid predators (natural pest control). Adults pollinate many flowers/fruits. Easy to attract/maintain with floral diversity; no hives needed.

5. **Butterflies & Moths (e.g., selected species or habitat for native analogs)** — Biodiversity enhancers.  
   Attract via edible/nectar flowers (borage, calendula). Add visual beauty and morale (Leg 5 QoL). Some larvae may need host plants but can be tightly managed. Hummingbird moths noted in greenhouse contexts.

**Lower Priority / Future**:
- Hummingbirds (if nectar flowers scaled up — high morale but higher complexity).
- Controlled beetles or other insects if specific crops require them.
- Avoid ants (can disrupt) or bats (high mass/complexity).
