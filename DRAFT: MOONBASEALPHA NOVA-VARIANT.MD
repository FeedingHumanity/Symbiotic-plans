### Nova-Class Expendable Integration Overview
The Nova-class variant (expendable direct-ascent configuration) fits seamlessly by maximizing early payload. It enables single-launch delivery of heavy machinery while providing hulls/tanks as immediate resources. This accelerates physical setup for **Leg 1** (food security via rapid storage/ag infrastructure) and feeds directly into **Leg 2** (waste recapture/recycling) and **Leg 3** (terraforming via tunneling support), without changing any Leg's core purpose or the overall symbiotic closed loops.

#### 1. Accelerated Infrastructure Deployment (Primarily Supports Leg 1 & Leg 3)
The baseline relies on heavy-machinery deployment, including Prufrock Tunnel Boring Machines (TBMs) for buried habitats that support food production stability. Standard reusable profiles are limited by return-fuel constraints. Nova-class expendables drastically increase initial payload, allowing immediate single-launch delivery of full-scale Prufrock TBMs, heavy nuclear/solar pyrolysis reactors, and large-scale agricultural infrastructure.

#### 2. Immediate Feedstock for "Hungry Hippo" Debris Recapture (Enhances Leg 2)
The proposal leverages the Hungry Hippo system under relevant sections of Leg 2 (and supporting early operations). A landed expendable Nova-class Starship provides a permanent ~50-meter stainless-steel hull as a massive stockpile of high-grade aerospace steel. The Hungry Hippo system dismantles it into building brackets, airlock expansions, structural casings, and other resources—closing the material loop faster.

#### 3. Repurposing Hull Sections into Biochar Storage and Root Cellars (Directly Strengthens Leg 1)
Leg 1 emphasizes low-energy anaerobic fermentation chambers, genetic vaults, and biochar-enhanced root cellars for humidity control and food supply stability. Empty propellant tanks from the expended Nova Starship deliver pre-fabricated, rigid, airtight volumes. Once cleaned and lined with biochar, they serve as instant massive root cellars, crop processing zones, or protective shielding barriers when buried under regolith—reducing early excavation needs while bootstrapping food security.
